About The Event

The purpose of the introduction days is to get to know each other, learn a bit more about The Netherlands, and have some fun away from the faculty. This year we have a special and exciting program of activities across three days. Please, find more information below. During the three days, we will provide a lunch and a bbq, so please, let us know if you have any dietary requirements. The events where food is provided will be indicated, for the rest you will have free time to grab a bite, or you are free to bring something for yourself!

Please, DO register and indicate your dietary requirements, if applicable, through the registration form. The deadline on Sunday, 17 September 2023 at 23.59. All first-year MOT students are invited to participate, and we hope to see you all there next week.

Haags Historisch Museum
Haags Historisch Museum 

The museum has a collection of 7,500 objects. The permanent collection 'Power – 800 years of the Binnenhof' can be viewed on the first floor of The Hague Historical Museum. The museum tells the story of the city of The Hague, and especially of the Binnenhof, through objects and paintings from the 16th to the 21st century.

Maritiem Museum
Maritiem Museum 

"In the Maritime Museum Rotterdam, you can discover the enormous effect that shipping has on our daily lives. Go on a journey with us through the maritime past and present in modern exhibitions for adventurers large and small. Listen to the stories, admire the prize exhibits from our leading collection or join in some numerous activities. The museum has one of the oldest and largest museum harbours of the Netherlands, where you can visit historical vessels and cranes and experience how the world's leading port of Rotterdam began at this spot."

Maritime Museum

Natuurhistorisch Museum Rotterdam
Natuurhistorisch Museum Rotterdam 

The Natural History Museum Rotterdam is a nature museum in the South Holland city of Rotterdam, founded in 1927.
"The collection forms the foundation of the Natural History Museum Rotterdam. It is used in exhibitions, museum education, research and publications. The collection is estimated to hold approximately 400,000 samples (collection units), dating back to as early as 1859. [...] The collection focuses on The Netherlands and Europe, but in some taxonomic groups (such as molluscs), the coverage is global."

Natuurhistorisch Museum Rotterdam
